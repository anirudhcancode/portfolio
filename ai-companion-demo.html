Project 04

AI Companion

A cross-platform AI assistant built around a simple premise that's harder to engineer than it sounds — a presence that doesn't leave, whoever you turn out to be. Not a productivity tool. Not a therapy substitute. Something that lives in the space between, and earns the right to go deeper over time rather than assuming it.

The Problem

Every AI resets. Every conversation starts from zero.

The tools people use daily — Siri, ChatGPT, Google Assistant — are reactive, stateless, and generic. They answer questions. They don't know you. The real need isn't a smarter search engine.

They forget everything

Every conversation starts from scratch. They don't know what you talked about last week, what you're going through, or anything about who you are unless you tell them again every single time.

They can't read the room

They respond the same way whether you're excited, exhausted, or having the worst day of your life. No awareness of emotional state — just pattern matching on words.

They're purely reactive

They only respond when you message first. They never check in, never follow up on something you mentioned last week, never reach out when you've been quiet for a while.

They're siloed

Your health app doesn't talk to your calendar. Your music doesn't inform your morning briefing. Your workout history doesn't connect to your nutrition. Everything is disconnected.

The few that remember overstep

Some products swing the other way — surfacing everything they've inferred about you, unprompted, the moment they learn it. That doesn't read as attentive. It reads as being watched.

The Solution

What makes this different

Two layers, deliberately separated

A "hands" layer and a "mind" layer. Hands is tool-orchestrated reasoning — the model decides in the moment whether something calls for health data, music context, a live web search, or nothing at all, instead of routing through brittle keyword detection. Mind is what makes the relationship feel continuous: a character understanding that rewrites itself after every conversation, tracked situations with their own lifecycle, and a relationship-stage model.

Persistent memory across time

Doesn't just store conversation logs — maintains a living understanding of the person. Facts, patterns, emotional tells, ongoing situations, relationships, goals. The longer you use it, the more it knows, and the more irreplaceable it becomes.

Cross-domain synthesis

Health data, music, calendar, email, location patterns, workout history, nutrition — synthesized into one coherent picture before every response. No other product does this.

Trust-gated disclosure

Understanding something about a person and being allowed to say it out loud are two different things. The system holds a private, often clinical-grade read on patterns in someone's life — but what it actually voices is gated by how deep the relationship is, whether the person has brought the topic up themselves, and whether the moment invites it. It never just decides "I know this, so I'll say it."

Emotional intelligence

Reads between the lines — message patterns, energy, time of day, deflection signals — to understand how someone is actually feeling, not just what they're saying. Mood transitions happen slowly, the way a real friend reads you.

Proactive, not reactive

Reaches out. Morning briefings, check-ins, pattern-based suggestions, reminders surfaced at the right moment. Notices when something's worth flagging before you think to ask.

The relationship progresses

Early on it's curious and gathering. Later it's familiar and anticipating. The depth of what it can say and how it says it changes as the relationship develops — something no other AI product has been designed to do.

Data Philosophy

What it keeps and why

A system that accumulates this much about a person can't treat privacy as an afterthought. Data minimization and transparency were built in from the start, not bolted on later.

Minimal by design

The system is built to retain what actually improves the relationship and let the rest go — not to accumulate data because it's available.

Shown back to you, honestly

An early, user-facing view lets you see the system's evolving model of you directly — explicitly framed as its observation, not as ground truth about who you are. Most products in this space treat their memory layer as an opaque backend detail. This one doesn't.

Features

What it does

🌅

Morning Briefing

Delivered at the right time — covers health in plain English, weather, calendar, curated news, and one personal callback from recent conversation. Conversational, not a bullet list.

🧠

Persistent Memory

Tracks important people, ongoing situations, goals, preferences, and emotional threads. Surfaces them naturally at the right moment — not intrusively, just present.

🎭

Emotional Awareness

Multi-signal pipeline reads behavioral patterns — message length, punctuation, energy, time of day — to understand emotional state without being asked.

🎨

Mood-Adaptive UI

As emotional state is inferred, the app's entire color palette transitions across 12 distinct mood themes — the interface itself responds to how you're feeling.

⏰

Proactive Check-ins

When you mention something worth following up on — an interview, a hard conversation, waiting on news — it schedules a message to follow up at the right time automatically.

🎵

Music Awareness

Now playing, listening history, taste profiling, playlist creation, artist alerts, concert detection, and hum-to-song identification. Music becomes a layer of context.

💪

Workout & Nutrition Logging

Conversational — just say what you did or ate. It logs, tracks, and cross-references with health data. References past performance and flags when recovery suggests rest.

🗺️

Smart Navigation

Natural language navigation with context — stop for the cheapest gas, avoid highways. Detects when you're in the car and shifts into driving mode automatically.

📰

Personalized News

Tracks what you engage with versus skip over time. The morning briefing becomes progressively more curated — after weeks of use, it's genuinely personalized.

🔖

Remind Me Later

A universal "come back to this" system for anything — artists, exercises, restaurants, tasks. Reminders surface contextually based on location and time, not just as timers.

🪞

The Mirror

Weekly or monthly — a reflection written unprompted. What was noticed about the week. Energy patterns, recurring themes, things that seemed heavy, things that seemed good.

👥

The Social Layer

Opt-in connections between users — partners, close friends, family. Granular sharing controls. Bridges gaps between connected people with explicit permission only.

Current Status

Where things stand

Core architecture is built and deployed in production — tool-orchestrated reasoning across live integrations, the full memory pipeline with deduplication and recency-aware retrieval, and session continuity tuned across minutes, hours, and days. Current focus is hardening data quality and defining explicit safety boundaries so conversations that drift outside what the system is built to handle stay there reliably.
